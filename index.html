PROJECT DEEP-DIVE: AUTOMATED TEA PACKAGING SYSTEM

Overview & Challenge

The 'Tawlefa' project in collaboration with Royal Herbs and iHub aimed to design an automated packaging machine to improve production efficiency for packaging tea bags into boxes.

As a mechanical designer, I developed the conceptual and technical CAD designs for Phase 1, securing 1st place and advancing the project to the physical prototype development stage.

Technical Design & Architecture

The system consists of 3 integrated mechanical modules modeled thoroughly to optimize space and material flow:

1. Input Conveyor Station: Responsible for feeding components smoothly.
2. Pick-and-Place Robotic Arm: Designed for precise positioning and box loading.
3. Rotary Table: Handles multi-station operations including box feeding and sealing.

PROJECT DEEP-DIVE: ASU RACING - URBAN CONCEPT EV

About the Competition

The Shell Eco-marathon is a global academic program focused on energy efficiency. We compete in the "Urban Concept" category, focusing on road-legal, city-ready vehicle designs.

My Journey & Leadership

My role evolved from a technical Member to Vice Leader, and currently the EV-VHD Team Leader. This journey has been a masterclass in Leadership, Time Management, and Teamwork under strict competition deadlines.

Technical Scope: Vehicle Handling (VHD)

As VHD Leader, I oversee three critical sub-systems through design, simulation, and integration:

1. Steering System: Designing for precision and minimal rolling resistance (Ackermann).
2. Braking System: Ensuring reliable stopping power and safety compliance.
3. Wheel Assembly: Optimizing hub, rim, and tire integration for maximum efficiency.
